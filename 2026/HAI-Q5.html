HAI-Q5
Top 5 Questions for Hyper/Super AI


An Open Event in GAI-HyperI 2026

July-November, 2026

Vision and Motivation

The next frontier of AI is not merely whether machines can think as A. Turing asked in 1950, but whether machines can surpass individual human intelligence through accelerative advancements, eventually toward hyper or super intelligence.

This is the vision behind Hyper/Super AI, tackled by IEEE HITC since 2020. With the rapid rise of Generative AI, large foundation models, reasoning models, multimodal systems, and autonomous agents, AI is moving beyond isolated models toward connected, collaborative, and adaptive intelligent systems. Hyper/Super AI represents this emerging trend, where humans, agents, tools, data, and cyber-physical environments are connected and coordinated to address complex real-world problems beyond the capability of any single human or model.

HAI-Q5: Top 5 Questions for Hyper/Super AI invites researchers, students, practitioners, and interested participants worldwide to submit profound, crucial, and forward-looking questions about the future of Hyper/Super AI. Inspired by two previous question-oriented events, Top10Qi (Top Ten Questions in Intelligent Computing/Informatics) in 2012 and Top10Cs (Top Ten Challenges for Smart Worlds) in 2015, HAI-Q5 continues the effort to identify key questions through broad community participation. A good question can reveal a hidden challenge, challenge an existing assumption, open a new research direction, and inspire collective discussion.
HAI-Q5 welcomes fundamental, forward-looking, and thought-provoking questions on the future of Hyper/Super AI.
Example questions for inspiration:
  • How should we define and measure “hyper-intelligence”?
  • How do we keep hyper-intelligence safe, controllable, and human-centered?
  • How can diverse human and AI entities be hyper-connected into a single trustworthy system?

HAI-Q5 Event Stages

Stage 1: Question Collection. Participants submit at least one and up to five concise questions through the online Google Form by September 10, 2026. Each question should be within 350 characters or about 50 words.

Stage 2: Question Analysis. The organizers will review, curate, and analyze all submissions by merging similar questions, removing unclear entries, identifying high-frequency keywords, grouping questions into major themes, and examining differences across regions, background fields, and formation ways.

Stage 3: Question Discussion. The final Top 5 questions, together with analyses and insights from all solicited questions, will be presented and discussed in a dedicated panel during GAI-HyperI 2026 in Melbourne, Australia.
