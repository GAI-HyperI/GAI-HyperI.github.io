HAI-Q5
Top 5 Questions for Hyper/Super AI


An open event in GAI-HyperI 2026

November 9-13, 2026, Melbourne, Australia

Vision and Motivation

The next frontier of artificial intelligence is not merely whether machines can think, but whether machines can surpass individual human intelligence through hyper-connected, collaborative, and self-evolving systems.

This is the vision behind Hyper/Super AI. With the rapid rise of Generative AI, large foundation models, reasoning models, multimodal systems, and autonomous agents, AI is moving beyond isolated models toward connected, collaborative, and adaptive intelligent systems. Hyper/Super AI represents this emerging direction, where humans, AI agents, tools, data, and cyber-physical environments are connected and coordinated to address complex real-world problems beyond the capability of any single human or model.

HAI-Q5: Top 5 Questions for Hyper/Super AI invites researchers, students, practitioners, and interested participants worldwide to submit profound, crucial, and forward-looking questions about this future. A good question can reveal a hidden challenge, challenge an existing assumption, open a new research direction, and inspire collective discussion.

Scope of Questions

HAI-Q5 welcomes questions related to the future of Hyper/Super AI, especially questions that are fundamental, forward-looking, and capable of inspiring research discussion.

Questions may address, but are not limited to:
  • Definitions, foundations, and evaluation of Hyper/Super AI;
  • Generative AI, large foundation models, reasoning models, multimodal systems, and AI agents;
  • Hyper-connected, collaborative, adaptive, and self-evolving intelligent systems;
  • Human-AI collaboration, hybrid intelligence, and collective intelligence;
  • Autonomy, creativity, reasoning, memory, planning, and self-improvement;
  • Safety, controllability, alignment, trust, privacy, ethics, and governance;
  • Hyper/Super AI for science, healthcare, education, smart worlds, robotics, work, and society.
Submitted questions should be concise and clear. They may focus on scientific foundations, enabling technologies, system architectures, evaluation methods, social impacts, ethical issues, or future applications.

Process: Collection, Analysis, and Discussion

HAI-Q5 will be organized through three main stages: question collection, question analysis, and panel discussion.

Stage 1: Question Collection
Participants submit up to five questions through the online form. Each question should be concise, preferably within 350 characters or about 50 words. For each question, participants will also select its formation way: Self-only, LLM-only, or Self + LLM.
This design allows HAI-Q5 not only to collect questions, but also to observe how future-oriented questions are formed by humans, LLMs, or human-LLM collaboration.
Stage 2: Question Analysis
The organizers will review, curate, and analyze all submitted questions. Similar or overlapping questions may be merged, while unclear or off-topic submissions may be removed.
The analysis may include:
  • Identifying high-frequency keywords and recurring concepts;
  • Grouping questions into major themes, such as definition, evaluation, collaboration, autonomy, safety, ethics, and applications;
  • Comparing questions from different geographic regions and research/background fields;
  • Analyzing the formation ways of questions, including Self-only, LLM-only, and Self + LLM;
  • Selecting representative questions that reflect deep, original, and widely relevant concerns.
Based on this analysis, the organizers will select the final Top 5 Questions.
Stage 3: Panel Discussion
The final Top 5 questions, as well as analyses and insights from all solicited questions, will be presented and discussed in a dedicated panel at GAI-HyperI 2026.
The panel will provide an open forum for researchers, students, practitioners, and invited speakers to discuss the selected questions, exchange perspectives, and explore future research directions for Hyper/Super AI.
